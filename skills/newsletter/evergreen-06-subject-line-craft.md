## Subject Line Formulas (Proven Patterns)

### Formula 1: Personal Discovery
"[Specific thing] that changed my [outcome]"
- "The one spice that changed my dal forever"
- "The tempering technique that saved weeknight curry"

### Formula 2: Practical Problem-Solving
"What to do with [common problem]"
- "What to do with leftover rotis"
- "When your garam masala tastes flat"

### Formula 3: Reassuring Guidance
"[Thing] without [fear/obstacle]"
- "Weeknight curry without the worry"
- "Homemade garam masala without guessing"

### Formula 4: Strong Opinion/POV
"[Thing] is/isn't [definitive statement]"
- "Curry leaves aren't optional"
- "This is the only dal recipe you need"

### Formula 5: Timeframe + Outcome
"[Timeframe] to [specific result]"
- "3 minutes to better curry"
- "Weekend curry in one pot, 45 minutes"

### Formula 6: Question Hook
"[Relatable question]?"
- "Why does restaurant curry smell better?"
- "Ever burn garam masala?"

## Character Count Guidelines

### Optimal Length: 25-50 characters
- Mobile inbox preview: ~30 characters
- Desktop inbox preview: ~50-60 characters
- Aim for 35-45 characters as sweet spot

**If >50 characters:** Ensure key information is in first 30 characters

## Always Do
- Reflect actual newsletter content accurately
- Use specific, concrete language over vague promises
- Create genuine curiosity without misleading
- Keep essential information in first 30 characters
- Test readability without context (does it make sense alone?)
- Use active voice when possible
- Include tangible benefit or clear topic
- Write 3-5 variations for comparison
- Reference Historical Files for high-performing patterns

## Never Do
- Promise content not delivered in newsletter
- Use clickbait tactics or false urgency ("You won't believe..." "Only 24 hours...")
- Include excessive punctuation or symbols (!!!, ???, ðŸ”¥ðŸ”¥ðŸ”¥)
- Write generic or template-sounding phrases ("Newsletter #47," "This week's update")
- Assume cultural references without context
- Use industry jargon without necessity
- Create confusion about newsletter purpose
- Use all caps or excessive emoji
- Make subject longer than 60 characters without good reason

## Subject Line Quality Checklist
